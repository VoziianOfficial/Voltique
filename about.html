Why Voltique exists

Simpler Request Matching

Electrical projects can involve different categories, timelines, provider terms, and property conditions. Voltique gives users one clear request path before they compare available local provider options.

Submit Details Browse Categories

Voltique exists to simplify the path from an electrical request to comparing local provider options. Instead of presenting itself as a contractor or direct service company, Voltique helps users organize their request details in a clear format. A homeowner can describe the electrical category, location, timing, and project concerns, then review available options from participating independent providers when available. The final decision remains with the user, and final pricing, scheduling, warranties, licenses, insurance, service terms, and work quality are handled by the provider the user chooses.

Platform clarity

Independent Matching Model

Not a Contractor

Voltique does not present itself as the company performing electrical work. The platform helps users organize requests and compare provider options.

Not an Electrician Company

Voltique does not send its own electricians, technicians, or crews. Participating providers handle their own scheduling, pricing, terms, and service work.

Not an Installer

Voltique does not install panels, wiring, EV chargers, lighting, or backup power equipment. Users compare options and choose whether to continue.

User stays in control

Review Before You Continue

Start with the electrical category, property details, timing, and project notes so the request can be routed more clearly.

Available participating provider options may vary by location, category, access, and provider availability.

Before choosing a provider, users should ask about pricing terms, scope, warranties, timing, permits, licensing, and insurance.

Submitting a request does not create a service agreement. The user decides whether to continue with any participating provider.

Honest platform standards

Trust & Transparency Principles

  • Voltique clearly identifies itself as an independent provider-matching platform.
  • No claim is made that Voltique performs electrical work directly.
  • Availability may vary by location, category, project scope, and provider participation.
  • Users should review provider pricing, scope, scheduling, and service terms before continuing.
  • Users are responsible for verifying provider license and insurance where required.
  • Final warranties, workmanship terms, and service agreements come from participating providers.
  • The user chooses whether to continue after reviewing available provider options.
  • Voltique avoids fake guarantees, fake reviews, unverifiable claims, and misleading direct-service language.
